# Step 8 Drift Warnings

## Template Drift

- do not let templates grow into automation chains
- do not let template payloads carry actions, tasks, or AI write parameters

## Dashboard Drift

- do not let dashboard summary reclaim template or recent responsibilities
- do not turn the workbench homepage back into a dashboard-first page

## Recent Drift

- do not let recent expand into a history log or audit timeline
- do not let recent become a second fact source

## Desktop Drift

- do not add desktop-only template systems
- do not add desktop-only recent or shortcut data sources
- do not let the shell grow native business pages

## Telegram Drift

- do not add Telegram template CRUD
- do not add Telegram workbench mode systems
- do not add Telegram-specific business semantics
- do not let Telegram drift into a management client

## Frontend Drift

- do not let browser state become the real template source
- do not add local-storage fallback semantics for workbench truth
- do not let UI shortcuts duplicate service semantics

## AI Drift

- do not let AI write to formal fact tables
- do not let AI outputs start sounding like formal system conclusions

## Knowledge Drift

- do not recenter the product around a knowledge graph
- do not let Knowledge become the visual or architectural center of the system

## Health Drift

- do not let Health drift into a medical decision tool
- do not let alerts or AI summaries read like diagnosis

## External Tool Drift

- do not reshape TraceFold around a stronger external note, graph, or automation tool
- do not let compatibility become the product center

## Engineering Scale Drift

- do not reintroduce plugin-system, queue, cache, or microservice assumptions into Phase 1 by default
