# Step 9 Chapter 5 Scope

## Purpose

Step 9 Chapter 5 relocks what Phase 1 still does not do.

This chapter is not future roadmap planning. It prevents tempting future directions from being written or implied as if they are already active Phase 1 defaults.

## In Scope

- Phase 1 non-goal review
- drift trigger definitions
- allowed exceptions and minimal skeleton clarification
- wording relock in docs, env examples, and lightweight guidance

## Out of Scope

- new product directions
- new business domains
- new automation capabilities
- graph-centered product redesign
- desktop-native business client work
- Telegram management-client expansion
- large code refactors

## Core Rule

For Phase 1, non-goals are formal boundaries.

They are not:

- optional suggestions
- “later anyway” defaults
- silent pre-approval for partial implementations
